Dim Sum

A style of Chinese cuisine prepared as small bite-sized portions of food served in small steamer baskets or on small plates.

Popular Items: Har gow (shrimp dumplings), siu mai (pork dumplings), char siu bao (barbecue pork buns)

Origin: Originated in Guangzhou (Canton), now popular worldwide

Mapo Tofu

A popular dish from Sichuan cuisine that consists of tofu set in a spicy sauce, typically a thin, oily, and bright red suspension, based on douban and douchi, along with minced meat, usually pork or beef.

Key Ingredients: Tofu, ground pork, doubanjiang, Sichuan peppercorns

Flavor Profile: Spicy, numbing (from Sichuan peppercorns), savory

Peking Duck

A famous duck dish from Beijing that has been prepared since the imperial era. The meat is prized for its thin, crispy skin, with authentic versions of the dish serving mostly the skin and little meat, sliced in front of the diners by the cook.

Serving: Typically served with thin pancakes, green onions, cucumber, and sweet bean sauce

Preparation: The duck is air dried and roasted in a hanging oven

Bubble Tea

Also known as boba tea, pearl milk tea, or tapioca tea, this tea-based drink invented in Taiwan has become popular worldwide.

Key Ingredients: Tea base, milk, flavored syrups, and chewy tapioca pearls

Variations: Can be served hot or cold, with a variety of flavors and toppings
