Pad Thai

A stir-fried rice noodle dish that's considered one of Thailand's national dishes. It's a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors.

Key Ingredients: Rice noodles, eggs, tofu, shrimp, peanuts, bean sprouts, and a special sauce

Origin: Created in the 1930s as part of Thailand's national campaign to promote Thai nationalism

Tom Yum Goong

A hot and sour soup that's aromatic, spicy, and packed with flavor. It's one of the most famous Thai dishes internationally.

Key Ingredients: Shrimp, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, chili peppers, and lime juice

Variations: Can be made with chicken (Tom Yum Gai) or as a creamy version (Tom Yum Nam Khon)

Som Tam

A spicy green papaya salad that embodies the flavors of Northeastern Thai cuisine. It's a perfect blend of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy.

Key Ingredients: Green papaya, tomatoes, long beans, peanuts, dried shrimp, chili, lime, and fish sauce

Serving: Often eaten with sticky rice and grilled chicken

Thai Tea

A sweet and creamy iced tea that's popular both in Thailand and around the world. It's known for its distinctive orange color.

Preparation: Made with strongly-brewed Ceylon tea, mixed with sugar and condensed milk, then served over ice

Variations: Can be served hot or with different types of milk, including coconut milk
